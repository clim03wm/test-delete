The Great Depression was one of the worst economic collapses in history, and people have debated its causes for nearly a century. The Hegemonic Stability Theory offers the clearest explanation.

The main reason for the Great Depression was that the United States failed to take on its role as the global leader, over-liberalized its market, and passed the Smoot-Hawley Tariff Act.

After World War I, the United States had both the wealth and the industrial strength to stabilize the global economy, but refused to do so. It declined to join the League of Nations and demanded repayment of European war debts instead of helping rebuild the international system.

Tariff policy made matters worse. The Fordney-McCumber Tariff of 1922 and the Smoot-Hawley Tariff of 1930 triggered retaliation and collapsed international trade.

The Great Depression shows what happens when global economic leadership disappears and markets are left to regulate themselves.
