Economy Philosophy:Cause of Great Depression
This post argues that the Great Depression can be best explained through Hegemonic Stability Theory. It claims the crisis deepened because the United States refused to take on global leadership after World War I, embraced excessive market liberalization, and adopted protectionist policies like the Smoot-Hawley Tariff. The article also traces how, despite its role in the collapse, liberal economic ideology later returned in the form of neoliberalism during the crises of the 1970s.
Double Edged Sword:Sweatshop
This post examines globalization as a double-edged sword, showing how free trade and multinational corporations fueled rapid growth in developing countries like China and Bangladesh while contributing to the decline of manufacturing and industrial communities in the United States. It explores how these economic shifts helped spark political frustration and the rise of populism in advanced economies.
